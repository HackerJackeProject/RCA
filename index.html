Raja Club Athletic (RCA)

Raja Club Athletic is a professional football club based in Casablanca, Morocco, that competes in Botola, the top flight of Moroccan football.

Founded on 20 March 1949 in the district of Derb Sultan,[1][2] the club has traditionally worn a green home kit since inception. Raja CA is a well known club for the success of its football section, very popular in and outside the country. Raja sits at the Raja-Oasis Sports center for training, The team has played its home matches in the 67,000 capacity Stade Mohammed V in downtown Casablanca since 1955.[3] Unlike most African sporting entities, Raja's members have owned and operated the club throughout its history.

The supporters of Raja Club Athletic (nicknamed the Curva Sud Magana) are the supporters of Raja de Casablanca, a club founded in 1949 in Casablanca. Raja is considered the people's club, hence the tifo made during the derby of the 2008-2009 season where it was written in Latin and in capital letters "Vox Populi" (the Voice of the Poor). The Raja public is renowned for its fervor in and out of the stands. The Curva Sud of Stade Mohammed-V, known as 'Magana', is the historical stronghold of the most fanatical supporters.
